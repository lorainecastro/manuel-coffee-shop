The Philippines is a archipelago of 7,641 islands blessed with some of Southeast Asia's most dramatic terrain. From the cloud-crowned summit of Apo to the sculpted rice terraces of Batad, these ten trails offer adventurers an unparalleled encounter with raw Philippine nature and living cultural heritage.

TOP 10 TOURIST TRAILS
01

Mount Apo

Davao del Sur / North Cotabato

The highest peak in the Philippines at 2,954 m, Mount Apo is a sacred volcano draped in mossy forests and home to the rare Philippine Eagle. Traversing fumaroles, sulphur vents, and boulder fields, this multi-day ascent is the ultimate Pinoy mountaineering rite of passage.

Extreme Multi-day Volcano UNESCO Buffer Zone
2,954 mELEVATION
~42 km loop
Oct – May
02

Batad Rice Terraces Trail

Ifugao, Cordillera

A living testament to Ifugao ingenuity carved 2,000 years ago, the Batad amphitheatre terraces offer a moderate hike through stone-walled paddies, crystalline waterfalls, and weaver villages. The viewpoints at sunrise rank among the Philippines' most breathtaking panoramas.

Moderate UNESCO Heritage Cultural
1,100 mELEVATION
~8 km
Mar – Jun
03

Mount Pulag

Benguet / Ifugao / Nueva Vizcaya

The "Playground of the Gods" sits at 2,922 m and is famed for its sea of clouds rolling over mossy elfin forests and dwarf bamboo grasslands. The Ambangeg trail is beginner-friendly, while the Akiki "killer trail" tests seasoned trekkers.

Moderate – Hard Sea of Clouds Protected Area
2,922 mELEVATION
14–28 km
Nov – Apr
04

Mount Pinatubo Crater Trail

Zambales / Tarlac / Pampanga

A post-apocalyptic lahar landscape leads to the stunning turquoise crater lake of the volcano that unleashed the 20th century's largest eruption in 1991. This 4WD-then-trek adventure across ash plains and river canyons is unlike anything else in the Philippines.

Easy Volcano Lake Day Hike
1,485 mELEVATION
~12 km RT
Nov – May
05

Chocolate Hills Trail

Carmen, Bohol

Over 1,200 perfectly conical limestone mounds turn a uniform chocolate brown during the dry season, creating a landscape from another world. The main viewpoint hike is short and family-friendly, making it the Philippines' most iconic easy adventure.

Easy Geological Wonder Family-Friendly
~50 mELEVATION
~1.5 km
Year-Round
06

Mount Kanlaon Traverse

Negros Occidental / Oriental

An active stratovolcano and the highest peak in the Visayas, Kanlaon's traverse through dense rainforest harbors endemic wildlife including the Visayan warty pig. The rim hike near the sulfurous crater demands respect and solid mountaineering experience.

Hard Active Volcano Biodiversity
2,465 mELEVATION
~30 km
Dec – May
07

Masungi Georeserve Trails

Rizal Province, Luzon

A conservation success story just an hour from Manila, Masungi's limestone karst formations are laced with a unique discovery trail of nets, rope bridges, and rock scrambles. A responsible ecotourism gem that funds reforestation of a once-degraded watershed.

Moderate Karst Eco-Tourism
~400 mELEVATION
~2.7 km
Year-Round
08

Osmeña Peak & Kawasan Trail

Dalaguete / Badian, Cebu

Cebu's highest point offers sweeping sea views over the Tañon Strait, and can be linked into a multi-day traverse descending to Kawasan Falls — one of the Philippines' most scenic waterfalls, a triple-tiered cascade of turquoise water ideal for canyoneering.

Moderate Sea Views Canyoneering
1,013 mELEVATION
~18 km
Nov – May
09

Mount Hamiguitan Range Trail

Governor Generoso, Davao Oriental

A UNESCO World Heritage Site containing one of only two Philippine pygmy forests — a surreal bonsai ecosystem of twisted, lichen-covered trees stunted by ultra-acidic ultramafic soil. A biologist's paradise hosting pitcher plants, critically endemic frogs, and the Philippine Eagle.

Hard UNESCO Heritage Pygmy Forest
1,637 mELEVATION
~22 km
Mar – May
10

Tarak Ridge Trail

Mariveles, Bataan

Often called one of the Philippines' best day hikes, Tarak Ridge delivers a dramatic open ridgeline with 360° views over Manila Bay and the South China Sea. A relentless but rewarding ascent through tropical forest that tests cardiovascular fitness and rewards with unforgettable sunsets.

Hard Day Hike Ridgeline Sea Views
~1,080 mELEVATION
~10 km RT
Oct – May

HIKER'S GUIDE
🗓️
Best Season

November to May is the dry season for most Philippine trails. June–October brings monsoon rains; many peaks close or become dangerously slippery.

📋
Permits Required

Most protected area trails require DENR permits obtained at the jump-off or ranger station. Book Masungi and Batad guides weeks in advance during peak season.

🏥
Safety First

Register at all trail registers. Hire a DENR-accredited guide for technical peaks. Carry a first-aid kit and a fully charged power bank at all times.

🌿
Leave No Trace

Pack out all waste, never pick plants or disturb wildlife, and camp only in designated areas. The Philippines' trails survive by the respect of those who walk them.

🎽
What to Bring

Rain gear (always), trekking poles, headlamp, water purification tablets, high-energy snacks, and sun protection. Layers matter even in the tropics at altitude.

🤝
Respect Culture

Many trails pass through ancestral domains. Ask permission before photographing community members, participate in local practices when invited, and support local guides and homestays.
